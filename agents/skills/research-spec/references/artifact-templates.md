# Artifact templates

Fill-in templates for the three spec files written into the `--from-spec` tmpdir
in Phase 4: `brief.md`, `plan.md`, `notes.md`. Fill **every** section with real
content from Phase 2/3 — never leave a heading with an empty body.

- [brief.md](#briefmd)

## Contract: exact strings the runtime parses

These are matched verbatim by `validation.py` and the publish step. Wrong
capitalization or a renamed heading silently breaks parsing or fails
`research validate`.

**brief.md** — for an **autonomous root-cause** case the validator requires these
section headers (warnings elsewhere, hard errors at completion/publish):

- `## Hypotheses`
- `## Known Confounders`
- `## Required Cross-Checks`

For other shapes these are recommended, not enforced. Always-useful headers the
publish step and status renderer read: `## Question`, `## Decision Or Deliverable`,
`## Scope`, `## Source Plan`, `## Success Criteria`.

**plan.md** — each thread is `### T<n>: <title>` followed by single-line bold
fields `**Field:** value`. A value must stay on **one line** (the parser reads to
the newline). High-priority threads (`**Priority:** high`) **must** carry all four
design fields or `research validate --design` fails:

- `**Discriminating Test:**`
- `**Strongest Rival:**`
- `**Completion Threshold:**`
- `**Cross-Check:**`

**notes.md** — the runtime and publish step mine these headers, so keep them:
`## Working Theory`, `## Dead Ends`, `## Open Questions` (plus `## Hypotheses`,
`## Evidence Log`, `## Leads`).

**report.md / status.md** are generated by `research init` — do not write them here.

## brief.md

`research init --from-spec` overwrites the `## Mode` block from the `--mode` /
`--template` flags and splices `## Source Registry` from the `--*-hint` flags, so
those two are placeholders below. Keep `## Known Confounders` and
`## Required Cross-Checks` for autonomous root-cause cases; drop them only for
non-root-cause shapes where they add nothing.

```markdown
# Research Brief

## Question

<the raw question, verbatim>

## Mode

- Selected mode: `<mode>`
- Research shape: `<template>`
- Created: `<YYYY-MM-DD>`

## Decision Or Deliverable

<the decision or deliverable this case supports — what the reader does with the
answer. One or two sentences, concrete.>

## Background

<context from Phase 2 discovery: what the target metric/phenomenon means and how
it is produced, the trend or event in question, relevant recent changes, and
organizational context. This is where the causal-system map from Phase 1b lands.>

## Hypotheses

- **H1: <claim>** [priority: high, status: untested]
  - Why plausible: <prior — what makes this a leading candidate>
  - Strongest rival: <the explanation that competes most directly with H1>
  - Discriminating test: <a check whose result points to H1 *or* the rival, not both>
  - Evidence needed: <what would count as enough to confirm>
  - Kill criterion: <the observation that would make you drop H1>
- **H2: <claim>** [priority: high, status: untested]
  - Why plausible: <...>
  - Strongest rival: <...>
  - Discriminating test: <...>
  - Evidence needed: <...>
  - Kill criterion: <...>
- **H3: <measurement / attribution / freshness explanation>** [priority: medium, status: untested]
  - Why plausible: <...>
  - Strongest rival: <...>
  - Discriminating test: <...>
  - Evidence needed: <...>
  - Kill criterion: <...>

## Scope

- In scope: <specific items the case will cover>
- Out of scope: <specific exclusions, each with a one-clause reason>

## Source Plan

| Source | What we get | Priority |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| <enabled source + specific objects> | <data this yields> | Primary / Supporting |
| ... | ... | ... |

## Key Dates

- <YYYY-MM-DD>: <launch, incident, config change, seasonal boundary, or external event>
- ...

## Known Confounders

- <effect that could mimic or mask the real signal: seasonality, mix/composition
  shift, routing or attribution change, instrumentation change, external platform move>
- ...

## Source Registry

<placeholder — `research init` replaces this body from the --*-hint flags>

## Required Cross-Checks

- <each high-confidence claim is confirmed in a second source family where possible>
- <the freshness cutoff is matched before live data is compared with any snapshot/export>
- <if the case pivots, preserve brief.md; record the new theory in notes.md and thread status in plan.md>

## Freshness Requirements

- <which facts must be verified live, and from which source>
- <which context may come from existing docs or local exports>

## Success Criteria

- <specific, checkable outcome — e.g. "top 2–3 drivers ranked with quantified
  contribution", not "a clear answer">
- ...
```

## plan.md

4–8 threads. Order by expected information gain (sharpest discriminating test
first), dependencies before dependents. T1 should **anchor** — confirm the trend
or event with hard numbers before anything tries to explain it.

```markdown
# Research Plan

**Case:** <question>
**Mode:** <mode> | <template>
**Created:** <YYYY-MM-DD>

## Threads

### T1: <short descriptive title>
**Priority:** high
**Source:** <enabled source family + specific objects/channels>
**Objective:** <the one question this thread answers>
**Main Explanation:** <current best explanation under test>
**Strongest Rival:** <best competing explanation>
**Discriminating Test:** <the concrete check whose result separates main from rival>
**Evidence Needed:** <what would count as enough evidence>
**Completion Threshold:** done when <...> / blocked when <...> / pivot when <...>
**Confounders / Freshness Risks:** <what could mislead this thread>
**Cross-Check:** <the second source or method that validates the claim>
**Depends on:** none
**Status:** pending

### T2: <title>
... (same fields) ...
**Depends on:** T1
**Status:** pending

## Dependencies

<one or two lines on thread ordering and why>

## Risks

- <threads that may not pan out, and the signal that would tell you to abandon them>
```

## notes.md

The research cockpit. Seed it with the working theory and hypothesis ledger from
Phase 3 so the first cycle starts informed — not empty.

```markdown
# Research Notes

## Working Theory

- Current best explanation: <1–2 sentences from domain context + hypotheses>
- Confidence: <low / medium / high, with a word on why>
- What would change this: <the observation that would shift the theory>

## Hypotheses

A lightweight ledger, not a form. One block per hypothesis from the brief.

### H1: <title>
- **Status:** active
- **Why plausible:** <rationale from discovery>
- **Strongest rival:** <competing explanation>
- **Discriminating test:** <what would move confidence>
- **Evidence so far:** <initial context, or "none yet">
- **Next check:** <the thread/source to start with>

### H2: <title>
... (same fields) ...

## Evidence Log

- **Claim:** <a source-discovery fact or tentative claim>
  **Source family:** <warehouse / experiments / docs / comms / observability / web / local / ...>
  **Source detail:** <table, page, thread, query, file, dashboard>
  **Supports:** <which hypothesis or confounder>
  **Caveat / freshness:** <what could make it stale or misleading>

## Dead Ends

- <anything already ruled out during discovery, or "none yet">

## Open Questions

- <questions that still matter for the case>

## Leads

- **<finding or thread to follow>** [high/medium/low]
  Why it matters: <impact on the case>
  Next: <specific next step>
  Sources to check: <source families>
```

## Worked thread example

A filled high-priority thread (from the bundled root-cause case) — note every
field is one line and the four design fields are present:

```markdown
### T1: Confirm the post-change export reliability break
**Priority:** high
**Source:** Snowflake (export_runs fact table) + local scheduler-change notes
**Objective:** Confirm whether export success rate dropped materially after the March scheduler change and localize the break.
**Main Explanation:** Queue contention from rescheduled heavy jobs reduced success rate.
**Strongest Rival:** The apparent break is volume growth or ordinary variance, not the schedule.
**Discriminating Test:** Compare pre/post success rate, failures, and queue minutes while holding scheduled volume flat, then confirm the same inflection in a second metric source.
**Evidence Needed:** A concentrated reliability drop with consistent timing across at least two sources.
**Completion Threshold:** done when the affected window and relative magnitude are explicit; blocked when the queue cut is unavailable; pivot when the break disappears after cross-checking.
**Confounders / Freshness Risks:** Partial-period March data and concurrent maintenance can distort the pre/post picture.
**Cross-Check:** Validate the change in both warehouse export metrics and a second derived queue metric.
**Depends on:** none
**Status:** pending
```
